#1 Dinner

Chinese

Kung Pao Chicken

Chinese: 宫保鸡丁 gōngbào jīdīng

Kung Pao Chicken is a famous Sichuan-style specialty, popular with both Chinese and foreigners. The major ingredients are diced chicken, dried chili, cucumber, and fried peanuts (or cashews).

watch

Ingredients

- 1 lb / 500g chicken thigh , cut into bite size pieces

Sauce
- 2 tsp cornflour / cornstarch
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1.5 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp Chinese black vinegar
- 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp sesame oil
- 1/3 cup water

Stir fry
- 2 tbsp peanut oil (or other cooking oil)
- 2 garlic cloves , minced
- 1 tsp ginger , finely chopped
- 6 to 10 dried chillies (adjust to taste)
- 3 green onions , cut into 2cm/ 3/4" pieces
- 1.5 tsp ground sichuan peppercorns
- 3/4 cup whole peanuts

#2 Dinner

Chinese

Hot Pot

Chinese: 火锅 huǒguō

Hot pot, or hotpot, is one of the most popular dishes in China, especially in Sichuan Province or Chongqing. People cook in and eat from a simmering pot of soup stock (broth) on a gas/induction hob in the middle of the dining table with foodstuffs and condiments around the pot. People can add and cook whatever they like in the broth.

watch

- 5g (2-3 pieces) Galangal

- ½g (9-10) Whole Clove
- 5g (about 2½t) Cumin
- 250g (1 cup plus 1T) Shaoxing Wine
- 150g (about ½-cup plus 1T) Píxiàn dòubànjiàng
- 15g (about 2T) Ginger
- 30g (about 1 large) Scallion
- 30g (3T plus 2t) Garlic
- 7½g (about a dozen) Dried Chili
- 15g (about 5t) Sichuan Peppercorn

#3 Dinner

Chinese

Sichuan Pork

Chinese: 水煮肉片 shuǐzhǔ ròupiàn

Sichuan pork, or 'poached pork slices', is a famous Sichuan cuisine dish. Pork, with a coating made from egg-white and starch to preserve its freshness and tenderness, is boiled in broth. The meaty broth is typical Sichuan cuisine, featuring a peppery and spicy taste. When eating, you'll find each piece of meat contains abundant juices with a fresh and fragrant spicy aroma.

watch

Ingredients

- 6 small pieces dried cloud ear mushrooms/4 dried shiitake mushrooms
- 500g pork fillet, sliced, cut into strips
- Pinch of salt
- 2 green shallots, green part thinly sliced lengthways, white part chopped
- 2 tbsp peanut oil
- 1 tbsp Lee Kum Kee Chili Bean Sauce Toban Djan
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp Chinese rice wine
- 60ml (1/4 cup) Massel chicken style liquid stock
- 70g (1/2 cup) canned bamboo shoots, drained, cut into matchsticks
- 2 tsp white sugar
- 2 tsp Chinese black vinegar or apple cider vinegar

Marinade
- 1 tbsp cornflour
- 1 tbsp water, cold
- 3 tsp light soy sauce
- 2 tsp Chinese rice wine
